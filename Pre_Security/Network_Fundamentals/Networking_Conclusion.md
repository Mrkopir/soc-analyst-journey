# Networking Fundamentals – Final Reflection

## Overview

During the completion of the Networking Fundamentals course, I strengthened and validated my knowledge in networking concepts. I gained a clearer understanding of how networks operate — from basic communication principles to tunneling mechanisms.

The course covered core technologies and concepts including IP addressing, MAC addressing, TCP/IP, UDP, LAN architecture, the OSI model, VPN technologies, firewalls, packets and frames, hubs, switches, routers, subnetting and subnet masks, ARP, DHCP, and ICMP (ping).

Additionally, I developed a solid understanding of how data is created, encapsulated into different protocol layers, transmitted across networks, and routed between systems.

## Technical Understanding

Throughout the course, I developed a deeper understanding of session establishment processes such as the TCP three-way handshake, as well as the differences between TCP and UDP communication.

I also studied firewall behavior (stateful and stateless), port forwarding mechanisms, and the fundamentals of network tunneling. This strengthened my ability to understand how traffic flows across networks and how connections are established, maintained, and terminated.

## Security Perspective

Most technical attacks — excluding social engineering — are conducted remotely over networks. Therefore, networking knowledge is fundamental for defensive cybersecurity.

Understanding how attackers perform reconnaissance, scanning, and connection attempts allows abnormal behavior to be identified at an early stage. Strong networking fundamentals enable earlier detection of malicious activity during the reconnaissance phase of an attack lifecycle.

This knowledge forms the foundation for effective monitoring, traffic analysis, and anomaly detection in Blue Team operations.

## Conclusion

The Network Fundamentals module provided essential groundwork for defensive cybersecurity.

Mastering these concepts establishes a strong base for progressing into more advanced Blue Team and security-focused studies.
